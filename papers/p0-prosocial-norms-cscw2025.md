## Abstract

Social norm interventions are used to promote prosocial behaviors by highlighting prevalent actions, but their effectiveness is often limited in heterogeneous populations where shared understandings of desirable behaviors are lacking. This study explores whether multi-agent systems can establish "virtual social norms" to encourage donation behavior.

## Research Question

Can multi-agent systems establish "virtual social norms" to encourage prosocial behaviors (specifically donation behavior)?

## Experimental Design

- **Type**: Online experiment
- **Setup**: Participants interacted with a group of agents to discuss donation behaviors
- **Conditions**: In-group agents vs. Out-group agents
- **Measurements** (pre- and post-discussion):
  - Perceived social norms
  - Conformity
  - Donation behavior
  - User experience

## Key Findings

### 1. Effectiveness of Multi-Agent Interactions
- Multi-agent interactions **effectively increased perceived social norms** and donation willingness

### 2. In-Group vs. Out-Group Effect
- **In-group agents** led to:
  - Stronger perceived social norms
  - Higher conformity
  - Greater donation increases
  
Compared to out-group agents

### 3. Social Identity Dynamics
- Social identity dynamics can be leveraged to promote prosocial behavior in virtual environments

## Implications for AI Multi-Agent Systems

1. **Group Identity Design**: The "social identity" of AI agents affects behavior change outcomes
2. **In-Group/Out-Group Dynamics**: Apply to AI agent-human interactions
3. **Norm Intervention Tool**: Multi-agent discussions as effective social norm intervention mechanisms

## Applications

- Behavioral change interventions
- Prosocial behavior promotion
- Virtual community norm establishment
- Human-AI collaborative decision making
