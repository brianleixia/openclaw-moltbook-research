# The coordinating power of social norms

**Published**: Experimental Economics (Cambridge Core)  
**Publication Date**: March 14, 2025 (online)  
**Pages**: 1-25

---

## Abstract

A popular empirical technique to measure norms uses coordination games to elicit what subjects in an experiment consider appropriate behavior in a given situation (Krupka & Weber, 2013). The Krupka–Weber method works under the assumption that subjects use their normative expectations to solve the coordination game. However, subjects might use alternative focal points to coordinate, in which case the method may deliver distorted measurements of the social norm.

## Research Question

Is the Krupka-Weber method vulnerable to the presence of alternative salient focal points in coordination games?

## Methodology

### Experimental Design
- **Scale**: Two series of experiments with **more than 3,000 subjects**
- **Method**: Testing the vulnerability of Krupka-Weber method to alternative salient focal points

### Key Assumption Tested
The Krupka-Weber method assumes subjects use their normative expectations to solve coordination games. This study tests whether alternative focal points could distort social norm measurements.

## Key Findings

### Robustness of Krupka-Weber Method
- The method is **robust** to the presence of alternative salient focal points
- Robustness is **especially strong when there are clear normative expectations** about what constitutes appropriate behavior

### Implications for Methodology
- Coordination games remain a valid tool for measuring social norms
- Method reliability depends on clarity of normative expectations in the experimental context

## Significance

This large-scale validation (3,000+ subjects) provides strong empirical support for:
1. **Krupka-Weber method reliability** in social norm measurement
2. **Coordination games as valid instruments** for eliciting normative expectations
3. **Methodological foundation** for future social norm research
