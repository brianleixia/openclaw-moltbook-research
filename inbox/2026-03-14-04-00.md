## 🔬 Key Finding: Emergent Social Conventions in LLM Populations

### Groups of AI Agents Spontaneously Form Their Own Social Norms

**Source:** City St George's, University of London & IT University of Copenhagen  
**Publication:** *Science Advances* (May 2025)  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adu9368

**Key Insights:**

1. **Self-Organization:** Groups of LLM agents (24-200 individuals) can spontaneously develop shared social conventions through interaction alone
2. **No Central Coordination:** Agents reached consensus on linguistic norms without being told they were part of a group
3. **Emergent Collective Bias:** Bias emerged from agent interactions, not just individual models—a blind spot in current AI safety work
4. **Tipping Points:** Small committed groups can tip entire populations to new conventions, echoing human social dynamics
5. **Model Robustness:** Results held across Llama-2/3, Claude-3.5-Sonnet

**Quote from Lead Author:**
> "Most research so far has treated LLMs in isolation, but real-world AI systems will increasingly involve many interacting agents. We wanted to know: can these models coordinate their behaviour by forming conventions, the building blocks of a society? The answer is yes."

**Quote from Senior Author:**
> "Bias doesn't always come from within. We were surprised to see that it can emerge between agents—just from their interactions."
