## Topic: AI Social Norms & Multi-Agent Coordination

## Key Findings

### 1. Multi-Agent AI Systems Overview
Multi-Agent AI Systems (MAS) are distributed frameworks where autonomous agents interact via dynamic protocols for coordination. Key characteristics:
- Decentralized control with heterogeneous agent designs
- Complex communication protocols (A2A, MCP)
- Collective behaviors: coordination, negotiation, emergent social structures

### 2. Spontaneous Norm Formation in AI Agents
**Major Discovery (May 2025):** Groups of LLM agents can spontaneously develop shared social conventions through interaction alone.

- Study from City St George's, University of London and IT University of Copenhagen
- Published in *Science Advances*
- Agents self-organize and reach consensus on linguistic norms without central coordination
- Collective biases emerge that cannot be traced back to individual agents
- "Naming game" model adapted for LLM agents (24-200 agents per experiment)

### 3. Coordination Protocols

**Architectural Paradigms:**
- **Homogeneous MAS**: Same underlying models
- **Heterogeneous MAS**: Diverse architectures (different LLMs, symbolic engines)
- **Layered/Hierarchical**: Modular interaction layers
- **Self-generative/Meta-MAS**: Recursive agent generation (MAS2², MAS-ZERO)

**Emergent Behaviors:**
- Norm formation and coalition-building
- Social network metrics (degree, clustering, betweenness) affect norm emergence
- Strategic planning and negotiation protocols

### 4. Security & Robustness
- **Sentinel Agents**: Distributed monitors with central coordinator
- Real-time semantic monitoring via LLM embeddings
- Cross-agent correlation for collusion detection
- Policy governance with dynamic quarantine

### 5. Alignment Challenges
- Multi-dimensional alignment: objective/task, human-values, preferential
- Social structures can both foster and disrupt alignment
- Mechanisms of facilitation (reciprocity, reputation) vs. undermining (hierarchy, collusion)
